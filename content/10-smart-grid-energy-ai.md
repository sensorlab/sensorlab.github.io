Smart grids are one of the most demanding settings for data-driven control: heterogeneous data, hard physical constraints and strong economic incentives. Our work here spans the full stack &mdash; from multi-agent deep reinforcement learning that [minimizes cost in energy communities](https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2026.3676334) and [improves energy autonomy of positive energy districts](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-12554-x), through [smart V2G charging with shared energy storage](https://doi.org/10.23919/SpliTech65624.2025.11091629), to the data infrastructure underneath: a [multi-region electricity knowledge graph](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-024-04310-z) and [natural-language interaction with a household electricity digital twin](https://doi.org/10.1109/SmartGridComm60555.2024.10738062). All deliverables are released as open datasets and code.
