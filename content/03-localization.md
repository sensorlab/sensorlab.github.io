Location-based services are now an inevitable part of emerging wireless infrastructures and business processes. Deep learning methods perform very well in wireless fingerprinting localization, and our work has produced datasets and pipelines based on [LOG-a-TEC](http://log-a-tec.eu/uwb-ds.html) traces. Recent contributions broaden this in three directions: [cross-technology localization with interpretable ML models](https://doi.org/10.1109/Balkancom65827.2025.11185957), a [configuration-first framework for reproducible, low-code localization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25692), and a new [indoor radio mapping dataset combining 3D point clouds and RSSI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.00494). We are also building [learned elevation models as a lightweight alternative to LiDAR for radio-environment-map estimation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05520).
