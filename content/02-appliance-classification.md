Automatic classification and segmentation of time series underpin many smart-infrastructure applications. Our early appliance-classification work using [shallow](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/355105512_Designing_a_Machine_Learning_based_Non-intrusive_Load_Monitoring_Classifier) and [deep](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/355105822_Learning_to_Automatically_Identify_Home_Appliances) ML is available as a [CaaS service](https://github.com/sensorlab/ApplianceCaaS). Building on this, [CARMEL](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.engappai.2023.107318) captures spatio-temporal correlations via time-series sub-window imaging and our [energy-efficient deep multi-label on/off classifier](https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2024.3382830) makes such models deployable on low-frequency metered devices. We are now exploring inherently interpretable architectures with [Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks for time-series classification](https://doi.org/10.1155/int/9553189), and a [network-science approach to granular time-series segmentation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17640) based on visibility graphs and community detection.
