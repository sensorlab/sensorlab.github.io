We study how teams of LLMs fail and succeed when scaled. [The Cost of Consensus](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00914) shows that isolated self-correction outperforms unguided homogeneous debate, and our forthcoming *scaling law* sorts any multi-agent configuration into **hard-ceiling**, **sublinear** or **linear** regimes &mdash; predicting the ceiling of 30-agent teams from a 5-agent pilot. Open code: [`llm-debate-dynamics`](https://github.com/sensorlab/llm-debate-dynamics).
